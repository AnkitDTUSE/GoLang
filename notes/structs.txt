-> Is GO a OOP language?
Yes and no; 
Go allows an object-oriented style of programming,
but it is not a traditional class-based object-oriented language.

-> GO has
Methods, Encapsulation, polymorphism

-> But doesnt has
Classes, Inheritance

-> To achive OOP 
Go relies on a unique combination of structures and interfaces 
to achieve the structural benefits of Object-Oriented Programming 

-> structs 
A struct (short for "structure") in Go is a user-defined type that 
allows us to bundle related fields of different data types together
